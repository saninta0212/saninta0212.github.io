Sambina Islam Aninta

Computational Biologist | PhD Student, University of British Columbia, Vancouver

I am a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering at the University of British Columbia, advised by Dr. Carl de Boer. My research focuses on understanding how deep learning models learn to predict functional consequences of genetic variants. My core work spans developing deep learning models for various functional genomics assays, constructing benchmarking frameworks for post-hoc interpretability methods, and developing generative models to study chromatin accessibility and DNA methylation.

My supervisory committee includes Dr. Peter Koo, Dr. Ali Bashashati, Dr. Xin Tang, and Dr. Sheila Teves.
